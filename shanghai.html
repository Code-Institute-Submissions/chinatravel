Shanghai

About

Welcome to China's largest city, Shanghai. Shanghai offers many sightseeing destinations for any kind of traveler. This city has many famous gardens and museums as well as innovative architecture. Some visitors come to Shanghai for the food, as Shanghai cuisine is considered to be one of China's top cuisines.

Explore Shanghai

1) The Bund

(Map location: A)

Strolling along The Bund is a must do while in Shanghai. It's home to amazing views of Pudong and its magnificent skyscrapers.

2) Longhua Temple

(Map location: B)

This Buddhist temple is one of the most beautiful ancient temples in the city.

3) French Concession

(Map location: C)

The French Concession is an interesting area that looks European, but is also Chinese. Its tree-lined avenues have hip cafes, restaurants, and boutiques.

4) Jing'an Temple

(Map location: D)

Jing'an means temple of peace and tranquility. This temple with interesting architecture is surrounded by shops and cafes.

5) Shanghai Old City

(Map location: E)

The Old City in Shanghai is everything you'd expect an old Chinese city to be like. It is definitely a great place to go to experience the history and culture of Shanghai. Within the ancient walled city, you can explore the buildings, temples, markets, and tea houses.

6) Shanghai Museum

(Map location: F)

The Shanghai Museum was founded in 1952 and is one of the most important museums of Chinese classical art. The museum is filled with ancient Chinese historical artifacts and is divided into eleven galleries.

7) Huxinting Teahouse

(Map location: G)

Just outside of the Yu Garden is the Huxinting Teahouse. This is an old and very famous teahouse in Shanghai, perfect for an afternoon tea.

1)Shiji Gongyuan/ Century Park

(Map location: H)

Century park is one of the biggest parks in Shanghai and is a nice place to find some peace in the busy city.

2) Yu Yuan/ Yu Garden

(Map location: I)

Filled with rockeries, pavillions, and magnificent pagodas, the Yu Garden is known to be one of the most famous classical chinese gardens. The garden was built in 1559 during the Ming dynasty and contains an impressive Jade Rock.

Traveling with kids? Here are a few extra fun things the whole family can enjoy together.

1) Nanjing Road

(Map location: J)

Nanjing Road is the main shopping street in Shanghai and can be a wonderful place to purchase souvineers. It is one of the world's largest and most famous shopping streets, attracting about one million shoppers each day.

2) People's Square & People's Park

(Map location: K)

People's Square and People's Park are located just south of Nanjing Road. There are always interesting activities here including; dancing lessons, Tai Chi, the famous marriage market, calligraphy, and small amusement park attractions.

3) Shanghai Disney

(Map location: L)

Shanghai Disney has many amusing attractions and is definitely something the kids will enjoy. This Disney may be smaller than the other Disney parks, but contains unique attractions that the whole family can enjoy.

4) Shanghai Ocean Aquarium

(Map location: M)

This aquarium has several exhibits featuring sea life from around the world. One of the most outstanding sections of the aquarium is the glass viewing tunnel, which is currently the world's longest!

5) The Oriental Pearl

(Map location: N)

The 468-meter tall Oriental Pearl Tower can be seen at the famous Bund skyline. The pearls contain sightseeing floors with a rotating restaurant, the Shanghai history museum, as well as other attractions.

When to Visit

Shanghai has a humid tropical climate and winters are cold, sometimes dropping below zero. Summer temperatures can reach as high as 32°C (90°F) in the month of July. Winter temperatures have an average low around 1°C (34°F). To avoid the hotest and coldest temperatures, the best time to visit would be in the spring (March-May) or in the fall (September-November).
