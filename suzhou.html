Suzhou

About

Affectionately called "the Venice of the East", Suzhou is known for its ancient gardens, pagodas, and water canals. Located just half an hour by train from Shanghai, Suzhou is a must visit while in China. See the ancient charm of old China in Suzhou!

Explore Suzhou

1) Pingjiang Road

(Map location: A)

Pingjiang Road is the perfect place to meander along the canals, enjoying the scenery, traditional music, and boutique shops.

2) Dongshan & Taihu Lake

(Map location: B)

Taihu Lake is one of the largest fresh water lakes in China with several small islands. There are many neat buildings and pagodas around the lake as well as several teahouses.

3) Suzhou Museum

(Map location: C)

One of Suzhou's most popular tourist destinations, the Suzhou Museum is located near the Humble Administrator's Garden and Pingjiang Road. You can see incredible ancient ceramics and woodcarvings here.

4) Silk Factory

(Map location: D)

Suzhou is also famous for their silk production. Tour the silk factories to learn the process of silk making, hand weaving, and silkworm raising- something that once was so important that it was punishable by death to know the secrets.

1) Humble Administrator's Garden

(Map location: E)

The Humble Administrator's Garden is the largest and most renound garden and is listed as a World Cultural Herritage site. It is one of China's four most famous gardens. This garden was initially built in 1509 and was a private garden. It is said that a government servant was frustrated in his career, so he intended to build a garden for his retirement. He thought this was a good simple life led by a humble man. That is how this magnificent garden came to have its name.

2) Tiger Hill Garden

(Map location: F)

Tiger hill has a number of historical sights that can be traced back over two thousand years ago. The pagoda at Tiger Hill stands on the summet of the hill and is located in the garden. This octagonal pagoda has a slight lean and can be seen from many of the other famous Suzhou gardens.

3) The Lingering Garden

(Map location: G)

The Lingering Garden is another one of the four most famous gardens in China. This garden is well known for its exquisite halls and buildings. The Lingering Garden was first built around four hundred years ago and has had several owners who did their best to perfect and maintain it.

4) Ouyuan

(Map location: H)

The Ouyuan Garden was built by a couple in the late 19th century. In this garden, everything you see from buildings to trees come in pairs, showing how this couple loved each other.

5) Master of Nets Garden

(Map location: I)

Master of Nets Garden is the smallest garden in Suzhou, but the way it is designed creates an illusion that the area is actually much larger than it is.

Traveling with kids? Here are a few extra fun things the whole family can enjoy together.

1) Take a boat ride on Suzhou's canals

Take a boat ride during the day or even at night! Boating through the waterways of Suzhou gives you the full experience of "the Venice of the East".

2) Suzhou Ferris Wheel

On the east side of Jinji Lake is the Suzhou Ferris wheel. It is said to be one of the largest in China. This sight has other attractions in addition to the Ferris Wheel, including a roller coaster for brave souls!

When to Visit

Suzhou's weather is mild and quite nice most of the year. It is best to visit this city from January to April since the weather is not too cold and the flowers are in full bloom. There are a number of festivals in Suzhou throughout the year:

  • Plum Blossom Festival: March 1-10
  • Tiger Hill Flower Festival: March-May
  • Tianping Hill Red Maple Festival: November
