About

Welcome to Beijing! Beijing is the capital city of China and is also one of the buisiest cities in the world. Home to many classic and modern chinese sights, Beijing is one of the most popular cities to visit in China. Explore Beijing

1) The Great Wall

(Map locations: A-C)

A trip to Beijing would not be complete without a visit to The Great Wall. There are several sections that are easy to access, including Mutianyu (A), Badaling (B), and Simatai (C).

2) The Temple of Heaven

(Map location: D)

Long ago at this cultural wonder, the Emperor used to come to pray for a good harvest season.

3) Tiananmen Square

(Map location: E)

Tiananmen, or the Gate of Heavenly Peace, lies right in the heart of Beijing. It is famously decorated with Chairman Mao's portrait.

4) The Forbidden City

(Map location: F)

Also known as the imperial palace, The Forbidden City is one of the most famous landmarks in China. This massive complex of palaces covers over 180 acres and contains more than 90 palaces and courtyards, 980 buildings, and over 8,000 rooms.

5) Wander the Hutongs

Hutongs are traditional narrow streets full of shops, cafes, and residences. You can get a little taste of what life in China is like here.

1) Summer Palace

(Map location: G)

This famous landmark is one of the most popular places to go in Beijing because of its beautiful structures and natural scenery. Kunming lake and Longevity hill are two of the essential landscape features of the gardens here.

2) Beihai Park

(Map location: H)

In this ancient garden, you will see the White Jade Pagoda which was built to honor the visit of the fifth Dalai Lama in the 1600s. Beihai bridge, Changfu temple, and Yong'an Temple are also found within the park and are well worth a visit.

3) Jingshan Park

(Map location: I)

Locared just across the road from the noth gate of the Forbidden City, this garden offers one of the most outstanding views of the city.

Traveling with kids? Here are a few extra fun things the whole family can enjoy together.

1) See the Great Wall at Mutianyu

This destination may already be on our list of things to do, but visiting the section at Mutianyu is a must for anyone visiting China. You can take a cable car up to the top of the wall and ride a toboggan down!

2) Beijing Chaoyang Theater Acrobats

(Map location: J)

The acrobat show at Chaoyang theater is particularly impressive! Shows filled with stunning costumes and incredible stunts are truly mind-boggling.

3) Seasonal Activities

During the winter months, the lakes in Beijing often freeze over which provide places for ice skating and sledding. When the lakes warm up, boating or playing by the side of the lakes are wonderful ways to spend the day.

  • Zhajiang mian- Beijing noodles in fried bean sauce

Where to Stay

You can book your hotel with booking.com. This website is one of our favorite search engines for locating the best deals on hotels. The best areas to stay in Beijing

  • Wangfujing: If you want to stay in a centeral part of the city, Wangfujing is a great place to stay. It is well located for visiting sights such as Tiananmen Square and The Forbidden City.
  • Qianmen: Located just south of Tiananmen Square, Qianmen is a convienent place to stay. Foodies will especially love this part of the city as there are many restauraunts and street markets.
  • Gulou/ Nanluguoxiang: These areas are trendy areas that offer everything you need, especially if you'd like to avoid a touristy area. You will enjoy getting lost and exploring the many cafes, shops, and alleys.

When to Visit

Beijing is a wonderful city to visit most times of the year. However, the winter can be a difficult time to visit with temperatures around the freezing point. To avoid large crowds and busy travel days, plan your trip carefully and not at the time of China's major holidays: Chinese New Year (Late Winter/Early spring) and National Week (The first week in October.)
