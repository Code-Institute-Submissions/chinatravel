Chengdu

About

Chengdu is the capital of Sichuan Province in western China and is well known for its delicious Sichuan food. This is the place to come and see China's most adorable and beloved animal- the giant pandas. Chengdu may be a large city, but it has a small town laid-back feel.

Nature Tour

Explore Chengdu

1) The Giant Pandas

(Map location: A)

See the famous Giant Pandas (and baby pandas) at the Chengdu Giant Panda Breeding and Research Base. You can tour the research center as well as learn about pandas and how to protect those still in the wild. This is something you don't want to miss while in Chengdu! You can even volunteer here. Help researchers record panda behavior, clean panda enclosures, and prepare a meal fit for a young panda. You can even have the opportunity to get up close and hold one of the black and white bears! The fee for volunteering helps ensure the preservation of these endangered animals.

2) Qingcheng Shan

(Map location: B)

Qingcheng Shan or Mount Qingchen is a UNESCO World Heritage sight and is known as one of the places Taoism originated. The natural scenery here is beautiful and magnificent. The Mount Qingcheng range has 36 peaks. Two of the peaks near the city, Front Mountain and Back Mountain, are definitely worth a visit.

3) People's Park

(Map location: C)

While this park may seem like a normal public park, it can be an interesting glimpse into the lives of the locals. You can often see retired people playing cards, dancing, and socializing. It's quite fun and relaxing to come spend time here.

1) Leshan Giant Buddha

(Map location: D)

The Leshan Giant Buddha is a huge statue that is carved in the side of a mountain. This Buddaha statue is 71 meters (233 feet) high and 24 meters (78 feet) wide. You can visit this destination on foot or on a sightseeing boat.

2) Sichuan Opera

The Sichuan Opera is one of China's oldest regional operas and originated about four hundred years ago. This kind of opera is different from western operas in they way that Sichuan opera has many circus like elements.

3) Kuan Zhai Alley

(Map location: E)

Kuan Zhai Alley is lined by ancient buildings and courtyards that are now converted into shops, tea houses, restaurants, and more!

4) Qingyang Palace

(Map location: F)

Built in the Tang Dynasty, Qingyang palace us a point of interest in Chengdu. It is one of the most famous palaces in China.

5) Drink tea at a traditional teahouse

Chengdu is one of the largest regions for producing tea in China. Visiting an old teahouse near the riverbanks or in the parks is truly an authentic experience.

You can't visit the Sichuan Province without trying some local dishes. Many local dishes are spicy and incredibly addictive. When ordering Sichuan style food, you can usually choose how spicy you would like your dish. Sentences like "要啦" (spicy) and "不要啦" (no spicy) will be useful!

  • Sichuan Hot Pot
  • Dry-fried Green Beans
  • Boiled Fish in spicy chili oil sauce
  • Kung Pao Chicken
  • Chuanchuan Xiang
  • Dandan Noodles
  • Mapo Doufu/ Grandma's Tofu

  • Chunxi Road: Chunxi Road is one of three on the list of China's top commercial streets. It's a great area for first time visitors to stay.

When to Visit

Chengdu has cold wet winters and sweltering hot summers, so it is highly reccomended to visit Chengdu in Spring and Fall. If you plan on seeing the pandas, visiting between August and December will give you a greater chance of seeing tiny newborns!
